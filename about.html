About Mental Health

Understanding the foundation of our well-being.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Did you know?

Mental health is just as important as physical health. They are deeply interconnected; poor mental health can increase the risk for long-term physical problems like diabetes and heart disease.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Mental health problems are a sign of weakness.
Fact: Mental health issues have nothing to do with being lazy or weak. Many factors contribute to mental health problems, including genes, physical illness, injury, or brain chemistry.
Myth: People with mental health needs can't work.
Fact: People with mental health conditions are just as productive as other employees. Employers who hire people with mental health conditions report good attendance and punctuality.

Why Awareness Matters

Awareness is the first step toward change. By talking openly about mental health, we:

  • Reduce the stigma that prevents people from seeking help.
  • Encourage early intervention and support.
  • Create a more empathetic and inclusive society.
  • Save lives by providing resources to those in crisis.
