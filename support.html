Get Support

You deserve help, and it is available to you right now. Let us guide you to the right resources.

If you are going through a difficult time, please know that you are not alone. Whatever you are feeling right now is valid, and seeking help is one of the bravest things you can do. There are people who care about you and want to help.

Emergency Support

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or experiencing a mental health crisis, please use one of these resources right away.

🆘 If you are in immediate danger, please call your local emergency number (911 in the US) or go to the nearest emergency room.

📞

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

Free, confidential support for people in distress. Available 24/7 via phone or chat.

Call or text: 988
💬

Crisis Text Line

Free crisis counseling via text message. Trained counselors available around the clock.

Text HOME to 741741
🌍

International Association for Suicide Prevention

Provides a directory of crisis centers around the world to connect people in need with local support.

Visit: iasp.info/resources

Finding Professional Help

A mental health professional can provide the guidance and tools you need. Here is how to get started.

🔍

Talk to Your Doctor

Your primary care provider can be a great first step. They can assess your situation, provide referrals, and discuss treatment options with you.

🧑‍⚕️

Therapists & Counselors

Licensed therapists offer talk therapy in various formats — individual, group, family, or couples. Many offer virtual sessions for convenience and accessibility.

🏥

Community Mental Health Centers

Many communities offer affordable or free mental health services. Check with your local government or search online for community resources near you.

💻

Online Platforms

Telehealth platforms provide convenient access to licensed professionals from the comfort of your home. Many accept insurance or offer affordable self-pay rates.

🤝

Support Groups

Peer-led support groups connect you with people who understand what you are going through. They are available in-person and online for almost every condition.

🎓

School & Workplace Programs

Many schools and employers offer employee assistance programs (EAPs) and counseling services at no additional cost. Ask your HR department or school counselor.
