5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

This sensory awareness exercise helps bring you back to the present moment when feeling overwhelmed or anxious.

1

5 Things You Can See

Look around you and mentally name five things you can see. It could be a book, a tree, a pattern on the wall — anything in your visual field. Take your time and really notice each one.

2

4 Things You Can Touch

Reach out and touch four objects nearby. Notice the texture — is it smooth, rough, warm, or cool? Feel the fabric of your clothing, the surface of a table, or the ground beneath your feet.

3

3 Things You Can Hear

Close your eyes for a moment and listen. What three sounds do you notice? It might be the hum of an appliance, birds outside, or your own breathing. Focus on each sound individually.

4

2 Things You Can Smell

Notice two scents around you. If you cannot smell anything immediately, think about two smells you enjoy — fresh coffee, rain, flowers, or a favorite meal.

5

1 Thing You Can Taste

Focus on one taste in your mouth right now. Take a sip of water or simply notice the current taste. This final step brings your full attention back to the present moment.

Daily Self-Care Checklist

Small consistent acts of self-care can make a big difference. Check off each item as you complete it today. Your progress is saved automatically.

🌅 Got at least 7 hours of sleep last night
💧 Drank enough water throughout the day
🥗 Ate a balanced, nourishing meal
🚶 Moved my body for at least 20 minutes
📵 Took a break from screens for 30 minutes
🗣️ Connected with a friend or loved one
📝 Wrote down three things I am grateful for
🧘 Practiced a relaxation or mindfulness exercise

🧠 Mood Check-In Quiz

Take a quick, private mood assessment to understand how you are feeling right now. This is not a diagnosis — just a helpful check-in with yourself.

How well did you sleep recently?

Think about the past few nights overall.

How would you describe your energy level?

Think about how you have felt during the day.

How much are you enjoying things you usually like?

Hobbies, socializing, favorite activities, etc.

How anxious or worried have you been feeling?

Consider your overall anxiety level recently.

How well can you concentrate on tasks?

Work, reading, conversations, daily responsibilities.

How connected do you feel to the people around you?

Family, friends, colleagues, community.

Overall, how hopeful do you feel about the future?

Your general outlook on what lies ahead.
